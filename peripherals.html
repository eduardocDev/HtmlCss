Computer Peripherals

Peripheral Description
Keyboard An input device that allows users to enter text and commands into a computer. Keyboards typically have a layout of letters, numbers, punctuation marks, and special keys.
Mouse An input device that allows users to control the cursor on a computer screen. Mice typically have two or three buttons and a scroll wheel.
USB Stick A small, portable storage device that connects to a computer via a USB port. USB sticks are used to store and transport data, such as documents, music, and photos.
External Hard Drive A portable storage device that connects to a computer via a USB port or a Thunderbolt port. External hard drives are typically larger than USB sticks and can be used to store large amounts of data, such as videos and backups.
Headphones A device that is worn over the ears and used to listen to audio. Headphones can be wired or wireless and come in a variety of styles, such as over-ear, on-ear, and in-ear.
Memory Card A small storage device that is used in digital cameras, smartphones, and other devices to store data. Memory cards come in a variety of sizes and formats, such as SD, microSD, and CF.
Cables A wire or group of wires that is used to connect two or more devices. Cables are used to transmit data, power, and audio.
Joystick An input device that is used to control a computer game or other application. Joysticks typically have a stick that can be moved in all directions and a number of buttons.
Smartphone A portable device that combines a mobile phone with a variety of other features, such as a camera, web browser, and media player.
